## LinkedIn Post

**Headline:** How Solo Creators Are Using Local AI Models to Build $80M Companies Without Teams

**Body:**

In 2025, Maor Shlomo built a software platform that lets nontechnical users build applications by describing what they want to a chatbot. Within a month of launching, it generated nearly $1.5 million in revenue. Four months later, Wix acquired it for $80 million.

He did this alone. No team. No employees. Just AI automation.

Shlomo is part of a growing wave of solopreneurs using AI to do the work of entire teams. The U.S. Census Bureau counts 29.8 million non-employer companies generating $1.7 trillion in revenue—roughly 6.8% of GDP.

But here's the opportunity most are missing: **local models**.

Most solo creators rely on cloud-based AI services—OpenAI, Anthropic, Claude. These work, but they come with tradeoffs:

- Recurring costs that add up quickly
- Data privacy concerns when sending sensitive business data
- Dependency on internet connectivity
- Limited customization

Local models—running AI on your own hardware—solve these problems. You get one-time cost, complete data privacy, offline capability, and full control.

**5 Practical Automation Ideas Using Local Models:**

1. **Content Repurposing Pipeline** - Turn one blog post into Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts, newsletters (2-4 hours → 15 minutes)

2. **Customer Support Intelligence** - AI categorizes tickets, drafts responses, flags complex issues (5-10 hours/week → 1-2 hours review)

3. **Research Assistant** - Monitors sources, summarizes trends, generates briefs (3-5 hours/week → 30 minutes review)

4. **Code Review & Documentation** - Reviews code, generates docs, creates tests (2-3 hours/feature → 30 minutes review)

5. **Meeting Intelligence** - Transcribes meetings, extracts action items, generates follow-ups (1-2 hours/meeting → 15 minutes)

**Getting Started:**

Hardware requirements are modest. Most local models run well on 8GB RAM (CPU) or NVIDIA GPU with 8GB+ VRAM. Apple Silicon Macs work excellently.

Recommended models: Llama 3.1 (8B), Mistral (7B), CodeLlama, Whisper.

**Your 4-Week Plan:**
- Week 1: Set up Ollama, test prompts
- Week 2: Build one automation (content repurposing is easiest)
- Week 3: Integrate into daily workflow
- Week 4: Measure ROI and iterate

The solo founders winning in 2026 aren't just using AI—they're building systems that scale their capabilities without scaling their costs.

Local models are how you do that sustainably.

Pick one automation. Build it. Measure the savings. Then expand.
