# Marketing & Promotion Strategy - AI Automation for Solo Creators

## Campaign Overview
**Campaign Name:** "Local Models for Solo Founders"
**Objective:** Drive awareness and adoption of local AI models among solo creators and small teams
**Timeline:** 30/60/90 day rollout

## Target Audience
- Primary: Solo creators (1-person businesses)
- Secondary: Small teams (2-5 people)
- Technical level: Mixed (technical and non-technical founders)
- Budget consciousness: High (prefer free/affordable solutions)
- Key motivators: Privacy, cost control, offline capability

## 30-Day Action Plan

### Week 1: Launch & Foundation
- Publish blog post on website
- Share Twitter thread (8 tweets)
- Post on LinkedIn
- Create Instagram carousel
- Send newsletter blurb
- Set up tracking (analytics, UTM parameters)

### Week 2: Amplification
- Repurpose content into additional formats:
  - Short-form video (TikTok/Reels) summarizing key points
  - Podcast guest pitch (AI productivity, solo founder shows)
  - Community engagement (Reddit r/solopreneur, Indie Hackers)
- Engage with comments across all platforms
- A/B test headlines on Twitter

### Week 3: Community Building
- Host Twitter Spaces discussion on local models
- Create downloadable "Getting Started with Local Models" checklist
- Launch hashtag campaign #LocalModelsForSoloFounders
- Respond to questions and build authority

### Week 4: Optimization
- Analyze performance metrics
- Double down on best-performing content
- Create follow-up content based on engagement
- Plan 60-day expansion

## 60-Day Action Plan

### Month 2: Expansion & Partnerships
- **Content Expansion:**
  - Deep-dive tutorial series (each automation idea gets its own post)
  - Case study interviews with solo founders using local models
  - Hardware comparison guide (CPU vs GPU vs Apple Silicon)
  
- **Partnerships:**
  - Collaborate with Ollama community
  - Partner with local model tool providers
  - Guest posts on AI productivity blogs
  
- **Community:**
  - Launch Discord/Slack community for local model users
  - Weekly office hours for implementation questions
  - Share user success stories

## Brand Voice Guidelines
- **Tone:** Empowering, practical, data-backed
- **Style:** Clear, actionable, no fluff
- **Differentiation:** Focus on local models vs cloud AI
- **Authority:** Use real examples and data
- **Community:** Encourage sharing and collaboration

## Risk Mitigation
- **Technical complexity:** Provide beginner-friendly content and support
- **Hardware concerns:** Emphasize modest requirements and CPU options
- **Time investment:** Focus on quick wins and ROI measurement
- **Competition:** Differentiate with local model focus and practical implementation
